Calculator Help Guide

Project value & revenue

Current land value
The current market value of the land if sold today (before development). Shows if development adds more value than just holding/selling the land. Also used for LTV calculations by lenders.

Total as-completed value (GRV)
The total value of ALL lots created by the development, including ones you keep. Example: If creating 2 lots worth $3.7M each, GRV = $7.4M (even if you only sell one). Shows total wealth creation, not just cash from sales.

Number of lots (in project)
Total number of lots or dwellings in your development project. Typical range: 2-50 lots for small-medium developments.

Number of units for sale
Number of lots you'll actually sell. If keeping some lots (e.g., for rental), enter only the lots you'll sell. Cannot exceed total project lots.

Average sale price
Your estimated selling price for each lot/dwelling, based on comparable sales and market conditions. Tip: Use conservative estimates.

Gross Sales
Total revenue from selling lots = Units for Sale × Average Sale Price. This is the cash that pays the bank and covers costs. All costs and profit are deducted from Gross Sales to determine Residual Land Value.

Costs

Construction cost
All hard costs to complete the development: materials, labor, site works, etc. Typical range: $1,500-$3,500 per m² depending on quality and location.

Professional fees
Architect, structural engineer, civil engineer, project manager, and other consultants. Industry standard: 6-12% of construction cost.

Contingency
Reserve fund for cost overruns, variations, and unforeseen issues. Typical range: 5-10% of construction cost. Higher contingency if project is complex or you have less certainty.

Sales & marketing
Real estate agent commissions, marketing materials, advertising, staging, etc. Typical range: 3-5% of Gross Sales.

Other costs
Stamp duty on land purchase, legal/conveyancing fees, council application fees, etc. Typical: $20,000-$100,000+ depending on land value and complexity.

Finance Costs

Construction amount required (net)
The actual construction amount you need to borrow (before interest and fees). The calculator determines the total facility size needed to cover this plus interest and establishment fees.

Interest rate
Private lender interest rate per annum. Typical range: 8-15% p.a. for private development finance. Higher rates significantly increase finance costs.

Construction period
How long construction will take from start to completion. Typical: 6-18 months depending on project size. Longer builds = more interest costs.

Facility term
Total duration of the facility including construction and sales period. Must be ≥ construction period. Longer terms = higher interest costs but more time to sell.

Calculated facility size
The total loan amount needed, calculated using a gross-up formula to cover: Net construction funds, Interest reserve (capitalised monthly), and 3% establishment fee.

Finance cost
Interest calculated on 55% average utilization during construction, then 100% during the tail period. Interest is capitalised monthly (added to principal), so you pay interest on interest.

Establishment fee (3%)
Upfront fee charged by the lender, calculated as 3% of the total facility limit. This fee is built into the facility calculation (grossed up), so the lender receives their fee while still providing sufficient funds for construction.

Profit

Developer margin
The profit margin you want to achieve as a percentage of total sales (Gross Sales). Typical range: 15-25%. Most lenders require minimum 15% margin for finance approval. Higher margin = lower RLV (less you can pay for land).

Results & Metrics

Residual Land Value (RLV)
The maximum amount you should pay for the land to achieve your target profit. Formula: Gross Sales - All Costs - Developer Profit = RLV. If RLV < Current Land Value, the project is unfeasible (better to sell land as-is). If RLV is negative, it shows cash shortfall required.

Development Profit (Project Lift)
Total value creation = GRV - (Land Value + Construction Cost). Shows the total wealth created by the development project, including lots you keep.

Return on Equity (ROE)
Return on your land investment = Development Profit / Starting Land Value × 100%. Shows how much return you're getting on your land equity.

Forecast LVR (Loan/GRV)
Loan to Value Ratio calculated against the total GRV (as-completed value). Lenders assess risk based on completed project value. Typical: Private lenders may go up to 70-80% LVR on as-completed value.

Loan to Cost (LTC)
Shows what percentage of total project cost (land + construction) is being financed. Lenders may finance 80-100% of construction costs, but total LTC depends on land equity.
